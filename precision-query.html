Precision Query

Highly Accurate User Queries, Matchups to RFIs/RFPs...

Quantum Ontology makes precision query possible. All of the ingested data from documents, users’ profiles of interests and expertise, and other data entered by members, can be queried easily and precisely. Precision query saves quantum researchers, engineers and stakeholders time and it provides complete, accurate results without all the noise typical of a Google search, for example.

Stakeholders can easily request RFIs with precision from performers. Performers’ data can be easily retrieved with precision from the knowledge graph. Missing information can be acquired from performed by semi-automated generation of requests for information (RFIs). Furthermore, the Quantum Ontology makes it possible to semi-automatically generate requests for proposals (RFPs). Performers responses can be ingested into the knowledge graph for reuse, saving time and effort in the future.

Several datasets are to be deployed here, including many that are ready to now:

  • Quantum Algorithms – catalog of about 70 today and growing, including the software for executing them on quantum computers or simulators, such as Python and Julia programs, and performers reports on how well they work (benchmark results, etc.)
  • Quantum Bibliographic DB – access to a huge collection of peer reviewed research (potentially free to members)
  • Quantum News - from multiple feeds, with push to members based on profile selections
  • Quantum Performers - members with quantum science and engineering expertise (dataset currently has over 2000 subject matter experts’ profiles)
  • Quantum Prototypes/Products – part of the initial beta content here, from over 500 quantum organizations (commercial, academic and government)
  • Quantum Services – single sign-on, from this secure portal via point-and-click, to access quantum computing as a service (QCaaS), e.g., access to quantum computers and simulators from providers like AWS, Google, IBM, IonQ, Microsoft, D-Wave, Rigetti
  • Quantum Stakeholders – members in this Quantum Science Organization looking to fund R&D and prototype development (<TR9*), or looking acquire commercial off-the-shelf products (TR9*)
*TR- technical readiness levels 1 (immature) to 9 (ready for fielding/deployments).
