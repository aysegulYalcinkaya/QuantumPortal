Ontology Fragment Bidding System

Development of the quantum ontology (QPhysO) is no small feat. It relies upon completion of the physics ontology (PhysO), the mathematics ontology (MathEO), and others, underneath the international standard (ISO/IEC 21838). The reason: the Quantum Ontology inherits from them. The foundation for this has been established by Drs. Smith and Landgrebe, QMI Principals.

We are soliciting volunteers with relevant niche areas of expertise in quantum science and engineering, to contribute to the development of respective ontology fragments, from a list of ones pending to-be-done. This portal has User Tiers. When users in the Ontology Developers Tier login, their menu options include one to the user interface of this Ontology Fragment Bidding System.

The old saying holds here: the whole is greater than the sum of the parts. Experts can develop these fragments, relatively easily, given their knowledge; these fragments become part of the quantum ontology, and as such, have high impact on the advancement of quantum science and engineering. This is explained in depth in a forthcoming book and elsewhere.

Development of these fragments require proper training. This training is provided free of charge to those engaging in this ontology development initiative.

Volunteers can elect to be paid, in money, or in points. If money, the expert simply enters a bid in US dollars to be paid for the respective fragment, and a target completion date. If the bidder has questions or comments, she can be entered it in a box on right-side of the bid entry fields (on the portal page).

If the bitter elects to earn points, instead of dollars, the bidder can commence with the effort immediately. Otherwise, the bid goes to the QMI sponsors to decide on whether to fund it.
