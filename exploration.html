Exploration

Ease of use and capabilities

Ease of use is key to the success of any system; we are exploring ways to make this portal as easy as possible. For one, volunteers need to be able to easily understand where they can best contribute to the on-going Quantum Ontology development, in context of their respective expertise and stakeholder (funders) high-impact Quantum Use Cases.

Stakeholders are another user group. Each group has somewhat unique requirements. We are exploring ways to display of menu options match the user’s respective group functions upon login. For Stakeholder the functions are primary precision search and matchups of their requests for information (RFIs) with quantum providers prototypes and commercial products.

We are also considering to option of providing portal functions in User Tiers, from basic data ingest and editing, to a full system support. Users would be assigned a tier by system administrator, and upon login the system would launch the respective functions of that tier. If assigned to the Ontology developers tier, user would have access to a Workspace containing a full-function development environment.

We are also exploring the option of providing support to quantum scientists and engineers. This portal is built on AWS, and as such has direct access to quantum computing via the Bracket. This portal could also provide a somewhat complete library of quantum algorithms, and be potentially free of charge for selected users, such as gifted students.

When users in the Volunteers group login, their menu options can include access to our exploratory Ontology Fragment Bidding System. This idea may seem strange to some, but what it offers makes it worth considering, e.g., it matches user’s interests and expertise to parts of the Quantum Ontology that remains to be completed, and those parts that a) will likely have most impact on the advancement of quantum science and engineering, and b) the parts of the ontology most applicable to supporting high priority stakeholder requirements.

It is also important that volunteers be conveniently paid, in money, or in QMI points. Here is an approach: if money, the expert simply enters a bid in US dollars to be paid for the respective fragment, and a target completion date. If the bidder has questions or comments, she can be entered it in a box on right-side of the bid entry fields (on the portal page). If the bitter elects to earn points, instead of dollars, the bidder can commence with the effort immediately. Otherwise, the bid goes to the QMI sponsors to decide on whether to fund it. Points can be applied to corporate sponsorship status, or other perks that are defined elsewhere. Payments in dollars are transferred electronically to the bidder’s account. Both are transacted after delivery and validation of the Ontology Fragment. If the bidder has questions or comments, she can be entered it in a box on right-side of the bid entry fields (on the portal page). If the bitter elects to earn points, instead of dollars, the bidder can commence with the effort immediately. Otherwise, the bid goes to the QMI sponsors to decide on whether to fund it.

These exploratory activities are described elsewhere in more detail. What matters is that volunteers are provided support for easily identifying, and developing, the remaining Quantum Ontology parts within the scope of their knowledge, with as little effort and frustration as possible.
