Help develop the Quantum Ontology

Development of the quantum ontology (QPhysO) is no small feat. It relies upon completion of the physics ontology (PhysO), the mathematics ontology (MathO), and other ontologies conformant to ISO/IEC 21838. The reason: the Quantum Ontology inherits from all of these. The foundation for this architecture has been established by QMIâ€™S Dr. Smith and Dr. Landgrebe. All of these ontologies are currently in early draft stage, and we are soliciting volunteers with relevant niche areas of expertise in quantum science and engineering to contribute to their further development. Contributing will bring a number of incidental benefits; first, it will enable contributors to make their mark on the ontologies in question in their respective areas of expertise. All contributions will be acknowledged in the ontology files themselves, and contributors will have the opportunity also to contribute as co-authors to papers which are published on QMI ontology work.

We hope also to organize arrangements whereby we can offer payment not only for ontology contributions, but also for other sorts of work, including evaluation (scientific validation) of content contributed by others, and also editorial contributions. Contributors will also receive any needed training, which will be provided free of charge.
